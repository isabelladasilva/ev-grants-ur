Electric vehicle chargepoint grant for landlords: guidance for installers

The Office of Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) pays grants to landlords, social housing providers and property factors to help with the cost of installing infrastructure for electric vehicle (EV) chargepoints in their rental property car parks.

This page has details on the kind of property and the type of landlords who can apply for this grant, along with the requirements the installation needs to meet in order to get the grant.

Which grants can landlords get?

There are 2 grants to support residential landlords with the cost of installing EV chargepoints:

  • the Chargepoint grant for landlords pays towards the cost of buying and installing chargepoints for landlords’ tenants
  • the Infrastructure grant supports the cost of the infrastructure to charge at least 5 EVs and at least 1 chargepoint socket. This is a different grant from the one we are describing on this page. Find out about the Infrastructure grant. [link to Infrastructure guidance page]
What do we mean by infrastructure?

Infrastructure means all the civil works, engineering, cabling, and connections from the power source to the parking bay, so you can install for a fully working chargepoint.

If your customer already has the infrastructure and only needs to install chargepoints, they can apply for the Chargepoint grant for landlords.

Your customer can also apply for the Chargepoint grant for landlords if they have already had the infrastructure grant and now want to install more chargepoints.

Who can apply for this grant?

Customers may apply for this grant if they are one of the following:

  • landlord with a property that you let or intend to let
  • right to manage (RTM) company
  • residents' management company (RMC)
  • company owning the freehold
  • company owning the building’s common areas including shareholders who are the leaseholders
  • leaseholder who has the freeholder’s consent to install chargepoints
  • property factor
  • private registered provider of social housing (PRP)
  • public authority (for example, government departments and their agencies, the armed forces, local governments, the NHS and emergency services)

What kind of properties are eligible?

The property must be located in England, Wales, Scotland or Northern Ireland. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man are not covered by this grant.

Customers can apply for:

  • single-unit properties such as flats and houses
  • landlords of residential property with property that you let or intend to let
  • multi-unit properties such as apartment blocks

This grant does not cover holiday rental properties.

Customers cannot apply for this grant if they use the property for their own business.

How much is the grant worth?

The grant covers up to 75% (including VAT) of the total cost of buying and installing EV chargepoint sockets.

The grant cap is set at £350 (including VAT) per chargepoint socket. This means you can get up to £350 for each EV chargepoint socket you install. If you install a twin or double chargepoint socket you can get up to £700.

Landlords of residential properties can install up to 200 chargepoint sockets per financial year.

What are my responsibilities as an installer?

Only OZEV authorised installers can claim the grant on behalf of the domestic customer. Apply to be an OZEV authorised installer.

You must:

  • tell your customer about their requirements for getting the grant
  • only use OZEV approved chargepoints
  • make sure you test all the installed chargepoints to check they work and meet safety requirements
  • keep all documentation about the installation, including schematic diagrams, installation and commissioning confirmations and certificates, photographs, customer declarations and signatures, dates of installation and the full address
  • notify the DNO of the installation in writing

You are responsible for managing EV fire risk

Landlords, property owners and property managers are responsible for making sure that they check their car parks for potential EV fires.

This means you must assess the parking area and put in place adequate and proportionate fire protection measures to guard against the risk to life from fire. You must also maintain these measures and update them as necessary when installing EV chargepoints. Your duties are covered in the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005.

How does the grant process work?

  1. The landlord will contact you about installing chargepoints.
  2. You survey the car park and produce a quotation that the landlord accepts.
  3. The landlord will apply for the grant.
  4. If their application is approved, you then have 90 days to do the installation.
  5. When you finish the installation you make a claim, adding proof that you have completed the job.
  6. Your invoice will include a discount to cover the grant amount.
  7. Once we have approved the claim, we will pay you the grant within 30 days.

See the step-by-step landlord chargepoint grant for installers

Grant rules and restrictions

You can only claim for one car park address in each application.

Each application should be for one job and have a single invoice.

A twin or double chargepoint counts as 2 chargepoint sockets.

The chargepoints you install must supply a minimum 3kW to each parking bay, regardless of how many parking bays are being used for charging at the same time.

The grant approved amount is valid for 90 days from the application submission date. If the work is not completed within 90 days you will have the option to extend the approval period for another 90 days.

OZEV reserves the right to take whatever action it thinks is appropriate if there is any evidence of:

  • grant requirements not being upheld
  • fraudulent activity
  • misuse of government funding by any party

  • Energy Savings Trust - domestic chargepoint funding in Scotland
  • EV Homecharge Scheme (EVHS) - for tenants only
