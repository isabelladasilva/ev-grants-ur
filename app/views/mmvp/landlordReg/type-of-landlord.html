What is a public authority or social housing provider?

A public authority is a government organisation that carries out tasks in the public interest or exercises the official authority it has been given. This includes government departments and their agencies, the Armed Forces, local government, the NHS and emergency services.

When OZEV is considering eligibility, it checks the public authorities listed in Section 33 of the LGA 2003, the Freedom of Information Act, Schedule 1, parts 1-3.

If you are still unsure you can find out if you fall into this category (opens in new tab).

A registered social housing provider is an English organisation that is registered with the government. It provides or intends to provide social housing in England. The provider is the landlord, or will be the landlord, of social housing.

Private registered providers (PRPs) are providers of social housing in England that are registered with the government and are not local authorities.
