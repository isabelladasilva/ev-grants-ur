How installers can claim the eletric vehicle infrastructure grant for residential car parks

The electric vehicle (EV) infrastructure for residential car parks, from the Office of Zero Emissions Vehicles (OZEV), helps landlords with the cost of putting infrastructure in place to charge EVs in their rented properties.

Go through these steps to check what you need to do to successfully claim this grant for your landlord or property managing customers.

  1. Step 1 Apply to become an OZEV authorised installer

    If you are not an OZEV authorised installer already, apply to become one.

    When you apply you will need to tell us:

    • • the Competent Person Scheme name and number
    • • your company name and address
    • • your head office address
    • • your business bank account details (bank, account name, account number and sort code)
    • • your work email address for communications from DVLA and OZEV
    • • your public contact information including, phone number, email address and website address (if you have one)
    • • the regions where you operate
    1. Apply to be an OZEV authorised installer

  2. Step 2 Get first-time access to the OZEV portal

    Once you are authorised, get a link to the portal and create a password.

    1. Get a link to the portal (opens a new tab)

  3. Step 3 Check your responsibilities as an installer

    OZEV authorised installers are expected to follow the grant rules, be aware of the relevant legal requirements and consider safety and accessibility requirements.

    Check your responsibilities in detail

    1. Check the list of approved residential chargepoint models
    2. View the guidance for installers, especially your responsibilities
    3. Make sure your installation meets all the regulations and requirements
    4. Make sure your claim meets the rules
    5. Notify the Distribution Network Operator (DNO) about the installation

  4. Step 4 Agree a quote with your customer

    You should be clear with your customers, so you both understand what work you will carry out. You both have to fill in parts of the claim. Both your claims need to agree in certain details, to get the grant.

    Before you start the installation you will need to:

    1. survey the site to check it has enough capacity for the number and type of chargepoints and future chargepoint infrastructure you will install
    2. check and confirm that you will make the installation in a private parking area
    3. agree on any infrastructure work you will need to carry out
    4. agree on how many EV charging parking spaces your infrastructure will support
    5. confirm the total number of chargepoints sockets you will install

  5. Step 5 Understand what landlords and property managers need to do to claim the grant

    Landlords and property managers need to make sure that their property and car park details and the details about the installation are based on your quote.

    Landlords and property managing agents start a grant application, then it's over to you to confirm their details and add proof that you completed the job.

    1. Check the EV infrastructure grant for residential car parks guidance for landlords (opens a new tab)

  6. Step 6 Have evidence to show you completed the installation correctly

    We will need evidence to show you have completed the installation correctly.

    To check your infrastructure work, we need a diagram with:

    • the layout of the car park and its marked parking spaces
    • the electricity meter that supplies the chargepoints
    • the installed chargepoints
    • which parking spaces have chargepoint sockets
    • connection locations for any future chargepoints
    • the parking spaces that you are claiming for
    • cable routes between the electricity meter and any future chargepoints
    • a key describing any symbols or abbreviations you use
    See an example of a diagram

    For approved chargepoint evidence, we will need to have:

    • the model of each chargepoint you installed
    • the serial number of each charge point you installed

    For photo evidence we will need to have:

    • photos of each chargepoint you installed
    • a photo of the car park, showing the chargepoints you installed
    • a photo of the property that car park serves

    They should be in jpeg, jpg, BMP, or PDF format and each one can be up to 5MB in size.

    We recommend that you give the photographs of each chargepoint a file name that is the same as the serial number.

    For billing evidence we need to have:

    • A copy of the invoice you gave to your customer

    If you raised more than one invoice, you will need to produce a final invoice that includes your total charges to your customer, including any VAT you charged.

    The invoice should clearly show:

    • the date you raised it
    • the customer’s details
    • the amount you charged your customer
    • the amount discounted because of the grant

  7. Step 7 Submit the grant claim

    Once your customer has submitted their part of the application it will appear on your applications page.

    When you have completed the installation, you can add your evidence and finish the claim.

    1. Sign in to your account to submit a claim
