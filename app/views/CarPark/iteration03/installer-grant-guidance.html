The electric vehicle infrastructure grant for residential car parks: Guidance for installers

The Office of Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) now pay grants to landlords, residents management companies, social housing providers, right to manage companies and property factors (in Scotland) to help with the cost of installing infrastructure for electric vehicle EV chargepoints in their rental property car parks.

This page has guidance on who can apply, how to apply, and the grant rules.

For guidance on how to apply for this grant, read the step-by-step guide for claiming the EV infrastructure grant for residential car parks for installers

Overview

The government wants to help businesses and organisations who rent, lease or manage residential properties install EV chargepoints in their car parks.

The EV infrastructure grant for residential car parks will give landlords and property managers money towards the infrastructure they need for chargepoints, as well as for installing the chargepoints themselves. They have to use an authorized OZEV installer to get the grant.

The grant means they do not have to install all the chargepoints the infrastructure allows. They can plan for the future by creating infrastructure now that can enable chargepoints later on.

There is a separate and different grant for installing EV chargepoints without infrastructure. This is the EV chargepoint grant for landlords.

What grants can landlords and property managers get for their car parks?

We have set up 2 grants to support residential landlords and property managers with the cost of installing EV chargepoints.

The EV infrastructure grant for residential car parks pays money towards the infrastructure your customer needs for chargepoints, as well as installing the chargepoints themselves.

To get this grant, the infrastructure must support at least 5 marked EV charging parking spaces. You must also install at least 1 chargepoint with 1 chargepoint socket

The EV chargepoint grant for landlords pays money towards installing chargepoints only. This is a different grant from the one we are describing on this page. Find out about the EV chargepoint grant for landlords.

If a landlord or property manager has already had the EV infrastructure grant for residential car parks and only needs to install chargepoints, they can apply for the EV chargepoint grant for landlords.

They can also apply for the EV chargepoint grant for landlords if they have already had the infrastructure grant and now want to install further chargepoints.

The infrastructure you need to install

The infrastructure needs to include:

  • a new electrical connection at a metered electrical supply point, such as a consumer unit or feeder pillar
  • a dedicated, safe, unobstructed route for electrical cabling from the electrical supply point to the installed chargepoints and any future connection locations
  • arrangements for the safe installation of an electric vehicle chargepoint

Infrastructure for future chargepoints

You must install cabling from a metered power supply to the future chargepoint locations so that someone can install a future chargepoint, at a future date, with the minimum amount of infrastructure work.

You can do this by installing, for example, a:

  • busbar system with connection locations for future chargepoints
  • concrete plinth or footing for a future chargepoint that will be connected to a metered electricity supply

You must also make sure there is enough power for all the chargepoints and future connection locations. Making sure there is enough power may include:

  • power upgrades to the local supply or network
  • applying load balancing solutions
  • upgrades to the buildingâ€™s electrical control room

Future chargepoint location routes

As part of the infrastructure, you must make dedicated, safe, unobstructed routes from the electrical supply point to each identified future connection location.

A future installer should be able to lay or insert any extra electrical cabling from the electrical supply to a chargepoint location, without the need for buildersâ€™ work. You can achieve this using any combination of electrical containment systems, such as electric cable:

  • ducting including drawstrings
  • trunking or conduits
  • trays and cable ladders

Earthing arrangements

The infrastructure must include all earthing arrangements any chargepoints need. You must also identify if future chargepoint locations need for any earthing arrangements.

What kind of properties are eligible?

The property must:

  • be in England, Wales, Scotland or Northern Ireland. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man can not get this grant
  • have an private off-street car park with at least 5 parking spaces, which is part of the property
  • be a multi-unit residential property such, as a block of flats, or an estate consisting of homes that share a common parking area.

If the property or car park is for mixed commercial and residential use, this grant will only pay towards parking spaces allocated to its residential units.

The landlord or property manager must be able to show you that they have legal entitlement to the parking space if it is separate from the property. They may prove this showing you Land Registry title deeds.

How does the grant process work?

  1. A landlord or property manager will contact you about installing chargepoints.
  2. You must verify that the car park is eligible for the grant.
  3. Your customer will then sign up for OZEV grants and make an application based on the quote for work that you both agree on.
  4. If their application is successful, you then have 90 days to do the installation. You can extend this, for up to 1 year.
  5. When you finish the installation, notify the relevant distribution network operator (DNO) and submit a BS7671 Electrical Installation Certificate.
  6. Make a claim, adding proof that you have completed the job.
  7. Your final invoice will include the total cost of the installation, including any VAT your charged, and the OZEV grant that reduces this total.
  8. Once we have approved your claim, we will pay you the grant to you.

What you can claim

Along with the cost installing infrastructure listed above, the grant may also pay towards the cost of:

  • any onsite software or hardware to manage and maintain the chargepoints
  • any communications equipment, such as wi-fi or cellular repeaters, that the chargepoints need
  • grid connections, but not any costs associated with grid reinforcement
  • concrete plinths or footings for future chargepoints
  • any associated signage
  • any associated reinstatement work
  • any associated vehicle barriers
  • the cost of chargepoint units
  • any additional electrical components or hardware required for the chargepoint units
  • civil engineering works
  • labour costs for installing chargepoints or infrastructure
  • project management and reporting costs
  • the VAT incurred by your customer. For enquiries relating to VAT, contact HMRC/VAT: general enquiries
  • site survey works when these lead to an installation you complete

The grant also covers the cost of an additional smart meter or current clamp device, as long as you install this at the same time as the chargepoints.

What you cannot claim

You cannot claim for:

  • any ongoing software support or services
  • interest charges, bad debts, profits and entertaining
  • administration costs
  • maintenance costs
  • inflation and contingency allowances that you add as an extra percentage, beyond the eligible costs. However, you can include reasonable inflation rates in your labour and material cost estimates
  • electricity provided by relevant infrastructure
  • ongoing back-office data services
  • any other ongoing operating costs for example, maintenance or communication
  • any warranty extension beyond 3 years
  • transport costs
  • marketing costs
  • project management and reporting costs
  • any other cost not listed as eligible

What are your responsibilities as an installer?

You must read:

  • Grant rules, restrictions and support for installers

  • Grant technical requirements and regulations for installers

Only OZEV authorised installers can claim the grant on behalf of the domestic customer. Apply to be an OZEV authorised installer.

The get the grant you need to follow these rules:

  1. Confirm who owns the car park.
  2. Only use approved chargepoints.
  3. Make sure your infrastructure and chargepoints meet or exceed the technical requirements.
  4. Do not claim for the same infrastructure or chargepoints more than once.
  5. Test all the installed chargepoints to verify they are fully functioning and meet safety requirements.
  6. Make sure there is adequate electrical supply for all the chargepoints and any future connections, once you apply load balancing technology.
  7. Take photo evidence that shows you have completed the work and followed the grant rules.
  8. Notify the DNO of the installation
  9. Submit a BS7671 Electrical Installation Certificate.

Consider chargepoint accessibility

When you install EV chargepoints, it is important to consider who may use them, particularly EV owners who may have disabilities or have mobility issues.
