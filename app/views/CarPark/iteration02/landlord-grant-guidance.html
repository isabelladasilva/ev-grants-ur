Electric vehicle (EV) residential infrastructure grant: Guidance for landlords

Find out what financial support residential landlords, social housing providers, property management companies and property factors can get help with the cost of the infrastructure for installing EV chargepoints in their car parks.

This page has guidance on who can apply, how to apply, and the grant rules.

Overview

The Office for Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) wants to help property owners, social housing organisations and property management companies who let or lease their properties to install EV chargepoints in their car parks EVs.

The residential infrastructure grant will give you money towards the infrastructure as well as installing the chargepoints.

You do not have to install all the chargepoints the infrastructure allows. You can plan for the future by creating infrastructure now that can charge EVs with chargepoints later on.

By infrastructure, we mean all the work you need to do to safely and properly connect chargepoints. It will need to be ready to install and use chargepoints without any further infrastructure work.

If you already have the infrastructure you need to install chargepoints, you can apply for the electric vehicle landlord chargepoint grant (opens a new tab)

Who can apply?

You can apply for this grant if you are a:

  • landlord with property that you let or intend to let
  • right to manage (RTM) company
  • company owning the freehold
  • company owning the building’s common areas including shareholders who are the leaseholders
  • leaseholder who has the freeholder’s consent to install a chargepoint
  • property factors
  • private registered providers of social housing (PRP)
  • housing association
  • public authority

You must be a UK registered company and registered for VAT or at Companies House.

If you are a registered company, charity or public sector organisation you can use your Companies House company registration number or VAT registration number to apply.

Your company registration number is the best number to use as it will speed up the application process.

Can you get the grant with your property?

Your property must:

  • be in England, Wales, Scotland or Northern Ireland. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man cannot get this grant
  • have a private off-street car park with at least 5 parking bays, which are connected to the property

You cannot get a grant if you need to lay cables over public land such as pavements, even if this is a temporary arrangement.

This grant does not cover holiday rental properties.

How much is the grant worth?

How much for infrastructure

You can get up to £500 towards for each EV the infrastructure supports charging at the same time.

How much for chargepoints

The chargepoint grant pays up to £350 towards the cost of buying and installing each chargepoint socket. Each chargepoint socket can charge an EV.

Example

To get the minimum infrastructure and chargepoint you would need to create the infrastructure to charge 5 EVs, and to install 1 chargepoint socket that can charge 1 EV.

5 x £500 + 1 x £350 = £2,850.

Grant limitations

As a landlord, you can make 30 applications each financial year. The financial year runs from 6 April to 5 April the next year.

Each application can be for up to a maximum of £30,000 and only be for one address.

Work with an OZEV authorised installer

Only OZEV authorised installers (opens in a new tab) can claim for EV residential infrastructure grant.

Installers must make sure that any chargepoint they install is listed on the OZEV approved chargepoint model list

Your installer will help you understand the installation process.

How the grant works

  1. Once you make your application, your installer will have 90 days to get the work done and to submit their part of the claim.
  2. If you or your installer need more time, you can request an extra 90 days for up to 12 months from the date you first applied.
  3. When your installer finishes the installation, they have to prove it is complete. This means that when your installer submits their part of the claim, they must send us proof that the work is done. This evidence includes a copy of your invoice and photos of the chargepoints they installed.
  4. OZEV pays the grant to your OZEV authorised installer who passes the discount on to you.

What you should do before you apply

Before you begin your application, you should:

  1. talk to an OZEV authorised installer (opens in a new tab) about your needs
  2. get a quote from your installer based on your requirements. It is important you follow their recommendation on whether you need infrastructure to be able to charge EVs
  3. understand the grant rules and restrictions [ANCHOR LINK]
  4. make sure that any chargepoint they will install is on the OZEV approved chargepoint model list
  5. workout how you will manage the chargepoints once they are installed, including maintenance and the electricity costs
  6. consider the accessibility needs are for EV owners who may be disabled or have mobility needs
  7. Get your installer’s OZEV installer number and start your application

You must have private parking

The space where you are applying to install chargepoints must have a private parking space. You must own the parking space or have the sole legal right to it.

The private parking may be connected to the property or separate from it.

You must be able to show you have legal entitlement to the parking space if it is separate from the property. You may prove this by showing us proof of Land Registry title deeds.

If you do not own the parking space or have the sole legal right to it, you cannot apply for the grant. This includes rented spaces.

Cables are not permitted over public land such as pavements. This is true even if the arrangement is temporary.

Create an account before you apply

To create an account, you will need one of the following:

  1. company registration number
  2. VAT registration number

You will also need:

  1. access to an email address
  2. UK mobile phone with signal as we will send you a security code

If you are a linked enterprise, you must apply through your controlling company using their company registration number.

How to apply for the EV landlord grant

  1. Find and choose an OZEV authorised installer.
  2. Confirm your chargepoint is on the list of OZEV approved chargepoint models.
  3. Get a quote from the installer.
  4. Review and approve your installer quote and get their OZEV installer number.
  5. Create an account and submit your application.
  6. Once the grant amount is approved the installer will be authorised to proceed with the work. They have 90 days to complete the job.
  7. After completing the job, the installer will submit proof of installation and claim for the grant.
  8. The full grant discount will be passed on to you.

Grant rules and restrictions

You can apply for one address per application. There may be multiple residences in one property that share the car park and multiple chargepoint sockets can be applied for in the same application.

Each application should be treated as one job and have a single invoice.

A twin or double chargepoint counts as 2 chargepoint sockets.

One application should be handled by one installer. If you use multiple installers you will need to submit multiple applications.

The grant approved amount is valid for 90 days from the application submission date. If the work is not completed within 90 days you will have the option to extend the approval period for another 90 days.

Other help you can get

There are other grants available for:

  • Businesses - Workplace charging scheme (WCS)
  • Energy Savings Trust - Domestic chargepoint funding in Scotland
  • EV Homecharge Scheme (EVHS) - for tenants only
