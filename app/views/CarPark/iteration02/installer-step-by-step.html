How installers can claim the residential infrastructure grants for landlords

The residential infrastructure grant, from the Office of Zero Emissions Vehicles (OZEV), helps landlords with the cost of putting infrastructure in place to charge electric vehicles (EVs) in their rented properties. Go through these steps to check what you need to do to successfully claim this grant for your landlord customers.

  1. Step 1 Apply to become an OZEV authorised installer

    If you are not an OZEV authorised installer already, apply to become one.

    When you apply you will need to tell us:

    • • the Competent Person Scheme name and number
    • • your company name and address
    • • your head office address
    • • your business bank account details (bank, account name, account number and sort code)
    • • your work email address for communications from DVLA and OZEV
    • • your public contact information including, phone number, email address and website address (if you have one)
    • • the regions where you operate
    1. Apply to be an OZEV authorised installer

  2. Step 2 Get first-time access to the OZEV portal

    Once you are authorised, get a link to the portal and create a password.

    1. Get a link to the portal (opens a new tab)

  3. Step 4 Check your responsibilities as an installer

    OZEV authorised installers are expected to follow the grant rules, be aware of the relevant legal requirements and consider safety and accessibility requirements.

    Check your responsibilities in detail

    1. Check the list of approved residential chargepoint models
    2. Make sure your infrastructure and chargepoints meet or exceed the EV infrastructure grant minimum technical specifications
    3. Check how to notify the Distribution Network Operator (DNO) of the installation

  4. Step 5 See what the grant could be worth

    The grant will pay:

    1. £500 of every bay the infrastructure can support charging at the same time
    2. £350 for each chargepoint socket you install.
      3. The maximum you can claim in each year is £30,000.

    Example of how much the grant could be worth

    To get the minimum EV infrastructure grant you would need to create the infrastructure to support 5 EV charging parking bays, and to install 1 chargepoint socket that can charge 1 EV.

    5 x £500 + 1 x £350 = £2,850.

  5. Step 6 Agree a quote with your customer

    You should be clear with your customers, so you both understand what work you will carry out. You both have to fill in parts of the claim. Both your claims need to agree in certain details, to get the grant.

    Before you start the installation you will need to:

    1. check and confirm that you will make the installation in a private parking area
    2. survey the site to check it has enough capacity for the number and type of chargepoints you will install. The infrastructure you create and the chargepoints you install must supply a minimum 3.5kW to each parking bay, regardless of how many parking bays are being used for charging at the same time
    3. agree on any infrastructure work you will need to carry out
    4. agree on how many EV bays your infrastructure will support
    5. confirm the total number of chargepoints sockets you will install now
    6. confirm the total number chargepoint sockets you will install now
    What do we mean by infrastructure?

    Infrastructure for chargepoints means:

    • cable routes, by which we mean channels for chargepoint cabling that run from the power supply to the chargepoint location
    • electrical containment systems
    • any associated reinstatement work
    • signage
    • earthing arrangements
    • any onsite one-off software purchases, but not ongoing software support or services
    • grid connections, but not grid reinforcement
    • concrete plinths or footings for future chargepoints
    • vehicle barriers
    • upgrades to existing electrical infrastructure
    • labour
    • project management costs

    Once you are satisfied that you have all the information you need and that the site is suitable for the installation, give your OZEV authorised installer number to your customer so that they can submit their grant application.

  6. Step 7 Understand what landlords and property managers need to do to claim the grant

    Landlords and property managers need to make sure that their property and car park details and the details about the installation are based on your quote.

    Landlords and property managing agents start a grant application, then it's over to you to confirm their details and add proof that you completed the job.

    1. Check the EV infrastructure guidance (opens a new tab)

  7. Step 8 Have evidence to show you completed the installation correctly

    We will need evidence to show you have completed the installation correctly.

    To check your infrastructure work, we need a diagram with:

    • the layout of the car park and its bays
    • the electricity meter that supplies the chargepoints
    • the installed chargepoints
    • which parking bays have chargepoint sockets
    • connection locations for any future chargepoints and how many parking bays these will serve
    • cable routes between the electricity meter and any future chargepoints
    • details of how you have terminated cables for future chargepoints
    • any earthing arrangements and the location of any future earthing arrangements, if these will be needed
    • a key describing any symbols or abbreviations you use

    Download an example of a diagram

    For approved chargepoint evidence, we will need to have:

    • the model of each chargepoint you installed
    • the serial number of each charge point you installed

    For photo evidence we will need to have:

    • photos of each chargepoint you installed
    • a wide angle photo of the carpark, showing the chargepoints you installed
    • a photo of the carpark, with the property it serves in view

    They should be in jpeg, jpg, BMP, or PDF format and each one can be up to 5MB in size.

    We recommend that you annote the photographs where possible with reference to your diagram.

  8. Step 9 Submit the grant claim

    Once your customer has submitted their part of the application it will appear on your applications page.

    When you have completed the installation, you can add your evidence and finish the claim.

    1. Sign in to your account to submit a claim
